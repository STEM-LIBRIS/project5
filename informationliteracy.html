What Are Information and Media Literacy?

Societal norms, economic stability, and quality of life are affected by the flow of information. Quality of information and media sources are crucial in times of instability, technological advances, and new discoveries of the world around us. It is important for members of a community to know how to seek out important information, where to search for this information, how to interpret it, and make informed decisions. Teachers and parents must be prepared to educate their students and children on how to analyse information they come in contact with, and be able to recognise resources of information that are questionable.

So what exactly are information and media literacy?

According to the American Library Association, information literacy is a set of abilities requiring individuals to “recognize when information is needed and have the ability to locate, evaluate, and use effectively the needed information.” To be information literate, then, one needs skills not only in research but in critical thinking (American Library Association, 2019).

On the other hand, media literacy is the ability to identify different types of media and understand the messages they're sending (Common Sense Media, n.d.). Media Literacy education – which teaches students to apply critical thinking to media messages and to use media to create their own messages – is a key 21st century skill. Media Literacy is critical to the health and well-being of America’s children, as well as to their future participation in the civic and economic life of our democracy (Media Literacy Now, Inc., 2020).
