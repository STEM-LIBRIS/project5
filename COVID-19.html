Where to Find Reliable Information

The COVID-19 pandemic has demonstrated the need for reliable sources of medical information. A simple Google search will reveal both reliable and inaccurate information. It can sometimes be hard to tell the difference. The COVID-19 pandemic has resulted in rapidly changing medical information. The sources below are highly-regarded, trusted, medical information sources.

Public Health Agencies

Public health agencies are considered to be the "gold standard" for health information. The information on their websites is updated frequently and is highly reliable. The following public health agencies provide information at the international, national, and state levels. Because information about the COVID-19 pandemic has been rapidly evolving, these resources are particularly valuable.

World Health Organization (WHO)

Headquartered in Geneva, Switzerland, the World Health Organization (WHO) is an international public health organization serving 150 countries. Their website states, "Our goal is to ensure that a billion more people have universal health coverage, to protect a billion more people from health emergencies, and provide a further billion people with better health and well-being."

SOURCE: https://www.who.int/about/who-we-are

Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention is the national public health agency. Their headquarters is located in Atlanta, Georgia. Their mission statement reads, "CDC works 24/7 to protect America from health, safety and security threats, both foreign and in the U.S. Whether diseases start at home or abroad, are chronic or acute, curable or preventable, human error or deliberate attack, CDC fights disease and supports communities and citizens to do the same."

SOURCE: https://www.cdc.gov/about/organization/cio.htm

Florida Department of Health (DOH)

The Florida Department of Health (DOH) is headquartered in Tallahassee, Florida. Their vision statement reads, "Florida Health represents the vision that health belongs to everyone and every group—not just a state agency. It takes individuals, families, communities, and partnerships with local and state-level policy makers and stakeholders to create a culture of health in Florida. While our agency is the spearhead for public health in the state, we are all stewards of health in Florida. Florida Health belongs to all of us."

SOURCE: http://www.floridahealth.gov/about/index1.html

Michigan Department of Health and Human Services (MDHHS)

The Michigan Department of Health and Human Services (MDHSS) is headquartered in Lansing, Michigan. Their vision is to "[d]evelop and encourage measurable health, safety, and self-sufficiency outcomes that reduce and prevent risks, promote equity, foster healthy habits, and transform the health and human services system to improve the lives of Michigan families."

SOURCE: https://www.michigan.gov/mdhhs/0,5885,7-339-73970_50649_50650---,00.html

Reliable Nonprofit Health Organizations

The following public health organizations are considered to be highly reputable.

The Mayo Clinic

The Mayo Clinic is headquartered in Rochester, Minnesota. The Mayo Clinic is highly esteemed among medical professionals. "Mayo Clinic is a nonprofit organization committed to clinical practice, education and research, providing expert, whole-person care to everyone who needs healing."

SOURCE: https://www.mayoclinic.org/about-mayo-clinic

The Cleveland Clinic

"Located in Cleveland, Ohio, Cleveland Clinic is a nonprofit, multi-specialty academic medical center that integrates clinical and hospital care with research and education."

SOURCE: https://my.clevelandclinic.org/departments/employer-healthcare-solutions/about

American Academy of Family Physicians

"Founded in 1947, the AAFP represents 129,000 physicians and medical students nationwide. It is the only medical society devoted solely to primary care. Today, family physicians provide more care for America’s underserved and rural populations than any other medical specialty."

SOURCE: https://familydoctor.org/about/

American Academy of Pediatrics

The American Academy of Pediatrics describes itself as, "an organization of 67,000 pediatricians committed to the optimal physical, mental, and social health and well-being for all infants, children, adolescents, and young adults.​"

SOURCE: https://services.aap.org/en/about-the-aap/

Altarum

Altarum is headquartered in Ann Arbor, Michigan. "Altarum is a nonprofit research and consulting organization that creates and implements solutions to advance health among vulnerable and publicly insured populations."

SOURCE: https://altarum.org/about-us/what-we-do
