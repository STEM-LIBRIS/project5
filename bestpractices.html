Teaching from a Distance

The abrupt transition to distance learning in March 2020 presented a unique set of challenges. The education world shifted dramatically. Below are resources to aid teachers as they reflect on this year's challenges and rebuild their personal learning networks in a post-COVID world. You will find TED Talks and podcasts, as well as scholarly articles.

TED Talks

As educators begin to process the rapid changes that have taken place recently, TED talks offer an opportunity to reflect on the practices that have served us well and will inform future teaching practices.

Title (linked) Description
Tough Teacher Truths: How to Improve Online Learning Mark Russo shares thoughts for teachers and families for the unique challenges presented by virtual learning.
How Schools Should Respond to COVID-19 Nora Flanigan shares her perspective as a Chicago high school teacher of the transition to distance learning.
A New 3 Rs for Education Jose Bowen presents a new paradign for education in the post-COVID, information-techonology-based world.
5 Needs That Any COVID-19 Response Should Meet Kwame Owusu shares his experiences with the Harlem Childen's Zone as they responded to the COVID-19 pandemic and its impact on the community.
How TED-Ed Is Helping Families, Students and Teachers Navigate the COVID-19 Pandemic TED-Ed@Home is curating and providing free video-based lessons to teachers during the COVID-19 pandemic.

Article Title (linked) Description

Trauma-Informed Practices

Lastly, recognizing that many students have suffered trauma during the COVID-19 pandemic, resources for trauma-informed practices are included below.

Article Title (linked) Description
Strategies for Trauma-Informed Distance Learning "To help educators use trauma-informed teaching practices in distance learning contexts, this brief offers strategies, with specific examples, for recognizing and responding to students’ social and emotional needs while teaching remotely."

SOURCE: https://www.wested.org/resources/trauma-informed-distance-learning/
7 things teachers can do to address student trauma – especially during distance learning "Before you can reach a student’s head to learn, you have to reach their heart and earn their trust. Educators should strive to be that support, especially during this pandemic and amid the civil unrest we are experiencing."

SOURCE: https://edsource.org/2020/7-things-teachers-can-do-to-address-student-trauma-especially-during-distance-learning/633512
Strategies for Trauma-Informed Distance Learning "To help educators use trauma-informed teaching practices in distance learning contexts, this brief offers strategies, with specific examples, for recognizing and responding to students' social and emotional needs while teaching remotely."

SOURCE: https://eric.ed.gov/?id=ED606091
Priority for Trauma-Sensitive Remote Learning: Keeping Connections Strong "The Trauma and Learning Policy Initiative (TLPI) is a collaboration of Massachusetts Advocates for Children and Harvard Law School whose mission is to ensure that all students, including those traumatized by exposure to violence and childhood adversity, succeed in school."

SOURCE: https://traumasensitiveschools.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/Trauma-Sensitive-Remote-Learning.pdf

SOURCE: https://www.tolerance.org/magazine/a-trauma-informed-approach-to-teaching-through-coronavirus

Professional development in the age of COVID-19 has changed, much like everything else in teachers' day-to-day lives. The resources above provide accessible, engaging information about best practices during this unprecedented time. As they enter the post-COVID world, educators will have learned new practices that will enrich instruction and engage students.
