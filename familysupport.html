Supporting Families During COVID-19

Parenting can be stressful, but parenting during a pandemic can provide many more new challenges. With schools and workplaces closed, many families have found themselves dealing with new, and often very stressful challenges. Provided here is a support page that offers families essential resources needed to navigate these new challenges. Focused on delivering the most supportive relief in these unprecedented times these resources include; stress-management, self-care, homeschooling and distance learning help.

Stress management and self-care

Faced with isolation and quarantine, families can struggle and find it difficult to keep a sense of calm. Remember, take a deep breath and know that we are all in this together. An important element in stress management is self-care. Think about it, if you are not taking care of yourself how then can you take care of those around you? Below is a specifically curated list of effective resources on managing stress for parents, and tips to take care of yourself.

Among these resources you will find podcasts by clinical psychologists, courses on stress management from the National Child Traumatic Stress Network and a helpful toolkit by The Center for Healthy Minds at The University of Wisconsin-Madison.


  • Parenting in a Pandemic: Tips to Keep the Calm at Home - Tips from the American Academy of Pediatrics to help your family get through the stress of the pandemic.
  • Burnout and How to Complete the Stress Cycle - Unlocking Us a podcast by Dr. Brené Brown. In this episode she discusses with Drs. Emily and Amelia Nagocki about Burnout, what causes it and what it does to our bodies, and how we can move through the emotional exhaustion.
  • What Parents Can Do When They’re Stressed During COVID-19 - Behavioral Medicine and Clinical Psychology at Cincinnati Children’s provide parents with 7 tips for coping with stress during a pandemic.
  • This Week in Virology - A science podcast hosted by Vincent Racaniello with weekly clinical reports on COVID-19 by Dr. Daniel Griffin an infectious disease and immunology board certified internal medical professional.
  • Well-Being Toolkit and Resources During COVID-19 - The Center for Healthy Minds at The University of Wisconsin-Madison provides resources to support well-being like guided meditations and webinars.
  • Supporting Families During COVID-19 - Resources from the Child Mind Institute to help families cope with COVID-19
  • Effective Parenting Stress Management - The Center for Effective Parenting has resources on stress management as well as other helpful information for parents
  • Parenting and Taking Care of You - A discussion of the importance of taking care of yourself as a parent of a child with special needs.
  • PBS: You Are Enough - A friendly reminder to parents that they are enough, and under the pandemic not every day will be perfect.
  • Sleep is your superpower - Sleep scientist Dr. Matt Walker’s TED talk
  • Parent Guide to Helping Families Cope with COVID - A parent’s guide to helping your family cope with COVID-19 from the National Child Traumatic Stress Network.

Homeschooling and Distance Learning

As parents and caregivers transition into the role of their child’s primary educator there will be challenges and stumbling blocks to overcome that include navigating multiple online resources to keep children engaged. The change to homeschooling can be challenging. However, a little planning and setting routines can help. Below are resources that focus on strategies to help keep families and school schedules running effectively that include navigating digital classrooms, homeschooling methods and student engagement.
