Distance learning during COVID-19

We are a group of educational professionals pursuing the problem of distance learning during the COVID-19 pandemic through strategic collaboration. While simultaneously completing our degrees in The Master of Library and Information Science program at Wayne State University.

The purpose of this website stems from the collective objective of completing our final project for INF 7420 Website Development, a required class for each of our programs.

Located in the heart of Detroit, Wayne State University offers over 300 academic programs. Of those programs the School of Information Sciences is one of the best known. The Master of Library and Information Science (MLIS) degree is an accredited degree that focuses on preparing professionals for leadership roles in libraries and other information organizations.

She received a Master's degree in Ethnomusicology from the University of Sheffield in northern England. She has also worked as a clerk in a public library. She is currently pursuing her Master's degree in Library and Information Science with a school library endorsement from Wayne State University. She can be contacted at gr4325@wayne.edu.
Heather Kapanka Heather taught high school biology and physical science in Ferndale, Michigan. She has an interest in microbiology and infectious disease. She was an educational consultant for Macmillan/McGraw-Hill publishers where she provided professional development for kindergarten through eighth grade teachers in the midwest region of the United States. She is currently completing a Master of Library Science, along with a School Library Media Specialist Endorsement at Wayne State University. Heather can be contacted at hd0329@wayne.edu.
